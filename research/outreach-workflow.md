# Hiring Manager Outreach Workflow

## Strategy Overview

Don't just apply and wait. For high-priority roles, find the actual decision-maker and reach out directly on LinkedIn. The goal: earn a 10-15 minute call or a direct handoff to the right person.

### Why This Works (Data)

- LinkedIn cold messages get **7-15% reply rates** (2-3x higher than cold email)
- Personalized notes boost reply rates from 5.4% to **9.4%** (nearly double)
- First follow-up alone generates **~1/3 of all replies** — most people skip this
- Sequenced follow-ups improve conversion by **~49%** vs one-off messages
- Contacting the hiring manager *before or right after* applying is more effective than waiting

## Step 1: Find the Job

Identify a relevant role through job boards, X-ray searches (see `research/x-ray-searches.md`), or company career pages.

## Step 2: Identify the Hiring Manager

Use Google X-ray to find the decision-maker on LinkedIn. Replace `"Company Name"` with the actual company.

**General engineering leadership:**
```
site:linkedin.com/in ("Head of Engineering" OR "VP Engineering" OR "Director of Engineering" OR CTO OR "Engineering Manager") "Company Name"
```

**AI / ML leadership:**
```
site:linkedin.com/in ("Head of AI" OR "AI Lead" OR "ML Engineering Manager" OR "VP of AI") "Company Name"
```

**Mobile leadership:**
```
site:linkedin.com/in ("Head of Mobile" OR "Mobile Lead" OR "Mobile Engineering Manager") "Company Name"
```

**Early-stage startups:**
```
site:linkedin.com/in (CTO OR "Co-founder" OR "Founder & CTO") "Company Name"
```

### Who to target

| Company stage | Typical hiring manager |
|---|---|
| Seed - Series A | CTO, Founder |
| Series B - C | Engineering Manager, Director of Engineering |
| Late stage / Enterprise | VP Engineering, Head of [Area] |

## Step 3: Send the Message

### Core Principles

1. **Lead with a fit hypothesis** — show you understand *their* product/problem
2. **One sharp differentiator** — pick the most relevant credential for this specific company
3. **Impact over skills** — anchor in outcomes, not buzzword lists
4. **Low-friction ask** — 10-15 minute chat, not 30 minutes
5. **Keep it scannable** — 50-120 words, 2-4 short lines, no big paragraphs
6. **One link max** in the first message

### How to use your key project

If you have a notable open-source project, treat it as a **credential, not a topic**. One clause, done.
- "I maintain {YOUR_KEY_PROJECT} ({PROJECT_STATS})" — then move on
- Tie it to their world: "{YOUR_PROJECT_ONE_LINER}"
- Don't explain the project in detail in the first message
- Don't link to it unless it's directly relevant to their work

### How to mention articles

Don't say "I write articles." Say: *"I've published {ARTICLE_COUNT} technical deep-dives on {YOUR_TOPIC}"* — this signals communication skill and thought leadership, which matters at VP level.

## Pre-Outreach Warm-Up

Before sending a cold message, spend 1-2 days engaging with the target's content:
1. View their profile (they'll see the notification)
2. Like a post or two
3. Leave a thoughtful comment on something they shared
4. Then send the connection request / message

This makes your name familiar before the cold message lands, and significantly increases acceptance rates.

## Outreach Templates

### Template 1: Open Role

Use when you've found a specific job posting.

> Hi {Name},
>
> Saw the {Role Title} opening at {Company}. Your {specific initiative/product area} caught my eye — I've been doing similar work at {YOUR_CURRENT_COMPANY}.
>
> I maintain {YOUR_KEY_PROJECT} ({PROJECT_STATS}, {YOUR_PROJECT_ONE_LINER}) and have shipped across {YOUR_RELEVANT_DOMAINS}.
>
> Would a quick 15-min chat make sense to confirm fit? If you're not the right person, happy to be pointed to whoever owns this.

**Adapt:** Swap the domain mention based on the role. For a mobile role, lead with your strongest mobile metric instead of the open-source project.

---

### Template 2: Networking (No Open Role)

Use when a company interests you but has no current openings.

> Hi {Name},
>
> I've been following {Company}'s work on {specific thing}. If you ever hire for senior engineers around {YOUR_TARGET_DOMAIN}, I'd like to be on your radar.
>
> Currently at {YOUR_CURRENT_COMPANY}, and I maintain {YOUR_KEY_PROJECT} ({PROJECT_STATS}) in the {YOUR_PROJECT_DOMAIN} space.
>
> Open to a 10-min chat? If not, happy to just connect.

**Adapt:** This is the only scenario where "happy to connect" works — you're not asking them to act on a req.

---

### Template 3: Warm Contact / Referral Request

Use when you know someone at the company or have a mutual connection.

> Hi {Name},
>
> Hope you're well! I'm exploring senior roles and {Company} is high on my list, especially around {area}.
>
> Quick context: I'm at {YOUR_CURRENT_COMPANY}, I maintain {YOUR_KEY_PROJECT} ({PROJECT_STATS}), and I've shipped across {YOUR_DOMAIN_BREADTH}.
>
> Would you be comfortable referring me or pointing me to the right hiring manager? Here's a ready-to-forward blurb:
>
> *"{YOUR_NAME} is a {YOUR_TITLE} at {YOUR_CURRENT_COMPANY} with {YEARS}+ years in {YOUR_DOMAIN_BREADTH}. Creator of {YOUR_KEY_PROJECT} ({PROJECT_STATS}). Interested in the {Role} — strong fit because of {1-2 relevant points}."*

**Key trick:** Including the forward-ready blurb removes all friction — the referrer doesn't have to write anything.

## Follow-Up Cadence

Each follow-up must add **new context or value** — never repeat the same message.

### Follow-up 1 (after 3-5 business days)

Add a new angle — mention a relevant article you published, a project update, or reference something new they posted:

> Quick bump on my earlier note. I recently published a piece on {relevant topic} that touches on what {Company} is building — thought it might be relevant. Still open to a brief chat if the timing works.

### Follow-up 2 — final (after another 7-10 business days)

Brief, light, graceful exit:

> Last note from my side. Still interested in {Company} — if timing's off now, no worries. Feel free to reach out anytime.

### Rules

- **Never send more than 2 follow-ups** — 3 total messages max
- Each follow-up provides a new reason to reply (new content, new angle, new value)
- If no response after follow-up 2, move on — persistence is good, pestering is not
- Track follow-up dates to avoid missing the window

---

## Timing

- **Best days:** Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
- **Best time:** 8:00-9:30 AM in the recipient's timezone
- Avoid Mondays (inbox overload) and Fridays (checked out)

## Anti-Patterns (What NOT to Do)

- Don't start with "I'm reaching out because..." — wastes the first line
- Don't paste your full background or portfolio
- Don't use more than one link in message #1
- Don't list all your skills — pick ONE domain match
- Don't ask for 30 minutes — 10-15 minutes feels low-commitment
- Don't message HR first — go directly to the engineering decision-maker
- Don't use "u" or "pls" — professional but conversational tone
